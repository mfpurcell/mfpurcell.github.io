About Marisa

I am a master's candidate at the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA) in the Department of Information Studies specializing in Informatics. I hold a bachelor's degree from the Univeristy of California, Berkeley in Rhetoric with a focus in Public Discourse.

My studies focus on the impact of information on internet companies and users. This includes but is not limited to: information-seeking behavior and information use; user-centered approaches to information system design; human-computer interaction; database design and management; and information policy. Topics that are of particular interest to me include: information architecture, UX, information production, knoweledge management, and asset management.

    Issue Paper

    The presence of disinformation on a digital platform has harmful consequences for both the user and company, as the discovery of disinformation can destabilize a user’s trust while jeopardizing a company's growth. This paper will focus on disinformation on crowdfunding platforms, using GoFundMe.com as a case study, and propose strategies to combat disinformation. As the internet enabled stronger lines of communication and eased access to larger audiences, crowdfunding capitalized on this phenomenon to widen their scope. The greater accessibility to donations, however, ultimately facilitated the perpetuation of fraudulent schemes, such as stolen identities or fabricated stories, on these platforms. Unearthing fraud is difficult when interactions with fraudsters are remote, but the mitigation and detection is essential to protect both users and companies. Fraudulent postings pose a financial risk to users, while undermining the mission of a crowdfunding company by ruining its reputation, draining its finances, and leaving it liable legally. In order to deter misinformation on crowdfunding sites, I will argue that user research, transparency, and regulation are essential components to maintain customer trust while detecting and mitigating fraud. As custodians of information, librarians and information professionals are trained to safeguard trustworthiness and curate content, making them valuable players in the fight against disinformation.
